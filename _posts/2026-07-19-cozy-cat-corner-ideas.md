## 1. Start with a bed they'll actually sleep in

Cats want to feel hidden and warm. A
[cozy cave-style cat bed](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=cat+cave+bed&tag=tamirugc20-20)
gives them both — and keeps the fur off your cushions. Place it against a
wall, never in the middle of the room.

## 2. Add a cat tree near the window

A window seat is cat television. A
[wooden cat tree](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=modern+wooden+cat+tree&tag=tamirugc20-20)
by the window covers three cat needs at once: climbing, scratching, and
judging the neighbors.

## 3. Or mount a window perch

Short on floor space? A
[window perch hammock](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=cat+window+perch+hammock&tag=tamirugc20-20)
suction-cups straight onto the glass. Sun + view = the most-used spot in the
house within a week.

## 4. Give them a proper scratching post

If you don't choose the scratching spot, your sofa will be chosen for you. A
[tall sisal scratching post](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=tall+sisal+scratching+post&tag=tamirugc20-20)
next to the sleeping spot works best — cats love to stretch and scratch right
after waking up.

## 5. Warm it up with a heated pad

For older cats especially, a
[self-warming cat pad](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=self+warming+cat+bed+pad&tag=tamirugc20-20)
turns a nice corner into a favorite corner. No electricity needed — it
reflects their own body heat.

## 6. Corral the toys in one basket

Cat toys spread like confetti. A small
[woven basket](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=small+woven+basket+with+lid&tag=tamirugc20-20)
in the cat corner keeps them in one place — and cats love fishing their own
toys out of it.

## 7. Set up a calm feeding station

Move bowls out of the walking path. A
[non-slip feeding mat](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=cat+feeding+mat+non+slip&tag=tamirugc20-20)
with raised bowls keeps the floor clean and gives your cat a quiet place to
eat.

## 8. Add a water fountain

Many cats drink too little. A
[cat water fountain](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=cat+water+fountain+quiet&tag=tamirugc20-20)
with running water gets even stubborn cats drinking more — better for their
kidneys, fewer vet visits.

## 9. Hide the litter box beautifully

A [litter box enclosure](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=litter+box+enclosure+furniture&tag=tamirugc20-20)
looks like a side table but hides the least pretty part of cat life — and
gives your cat the privacy they prefer anyway.

## 10. Grow a pot of cat grass

A small pot of
[cat grass](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=cat+grass+growing+kit&tag=tamirugc20-20)
in the corner aids digestion and keeps your houseplants safer from nibbling.

## 11. Go vertical with wall shelves

Cats feel safest above the room. A few
[cat wall shelves](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=cat+wall+shelves&tag=tamirugc20-20)
in a stair pattern turn an empty wall into a highway — and it looks like
modern art.

## 12. Keep a soft blanket just for them

One [soft pet blanket](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=soft+pet+blanket&tag=tamirugc20-20)
that smells like *them* makes the corner theirs. Bonus: it's the blanket you
wash, instead of your throw pillows.

---

**The takeaway:** start with the bed by the window and a scratching post
(ideas 1–4) — that covers sleeping, watching, and scratching. Add one comfort
upgrade a week, and within a month the sofa is yours again.
