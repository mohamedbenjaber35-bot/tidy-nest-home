## 1. Clear the counters first

Counters collect clutter fast. Corral your everyday tools in a single
[countertop utensil crock](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=countertop+utensil+holder&tag=tamirugc20-20)
and move everything else into drawers. Clear counters instantly make the whole
kitchen feel calmer.

## 2. Divide your utensil drawer

The junk-drawer feeling usually starts in the utensil drawer. A set of
[bamboo drawer dividers](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=bamboo+drawer+organizer+utensil&tag=tamirugc20-20)
gives spoons, spatulas, and whisks their own lanes so you can grab what you need
without digging.

## 3. Tame the under-sink chaos

Under the sink is prime real estate that usually goes to waste. A
[2-tier under-sink organizer](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=under+sink+organizer+2+tier&tag=tamirugc20-20)
works around the pipes and turns that dark cave into usable, easy-to-reach
storage.

## 4. Put your spices in a drawer

Spices lost in a deep cabinet get bought twice. A
[drawer spice organizer](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=spice+drawer+organizer+insert&tag=tamirugc20-20)
lays them flat so you can read every label at a glance — no more knocking over
five jars to find the cumin.

## 5. Decant staples into clear canisters

Flour, sugar, rice, and pasta look tidy and stay fresh in matching
[clear airtight canisters](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=airtight+food+storage+canisters&tag=tamirugc20-20).
You'll also see exactly when you're running low, which makes the shopping list
write itself.

## 6. Add a lazy Susan to deep cabinets

Corner cabinets swallow bottles and jars whole. A
[turntable lazy Susan](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=lazy+susan+cabinet+organizer&tag=tamirugc20-20)
spins everything to the front, so oils, sauces, and vinegars are always one
reach away.

## 7. Sort pot lids upright

Loose lids are the loudest, most annoying part of any cabinet. A
[pot lid rack](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=pot+lid+organizer+rack&tag=tamirugc20-20)
stands them on end so they stop sliding around and you can actually find the one
that fits.

## 8. Use the back of cabinet doors

The inside of a cabinet door is free storage most people ignore. An
[over-the-cabinet-door organizer](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=over+the+cabinet+door+organizer&tag=tamirugc20-20)
is perfect for foil, wraps, sponges, or a cutting board.

## 9. Bin up the fridge

Loose items get lost and go bad at the back of the fridge. A set of
[clear fridge bins](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=clear+refrigerator+organizer+bins&tag=tamirugc20-20)
groups snacks, drinks, and leftovers so you can pull out a whole bin instead of
hunting shelf by shelf.

## 10. Double your cabinet shelves

Half-empty cabinets waste vertical space. Add
[stackable shelf risers](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=cabinet+shelf+organizer+riser&tag=tamirugc20-20)
to create a second level for mugs, plates, or cans — instantly doubling what

## 11. Hang the tools you use most

Free up drawer space by hanging your go-to tools. A
[wall-mounted utensil rail](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=kitchen+utensil+hanging+rail&tag=tamirugc20-20)

## 12. Group snacks in a pull-out basket

A single snack basket saves you from ten little open boxes. A
[pull-out pantry basket](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=pull+out+pantry+basket+bin&tag=tamirugc20-20)

## 13. Organize wraps and foil in one spot

Foil, cling film, and parchment always end up in a tangled pile. A
[wrap and foil organizer](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=plastic+wrap+foil+organizer&tag=tamirugc20-20)

Organization only lasts if everything goes back where it belongs. A little
[label maker](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=label+maker+machine&tag=tamirugc20-20)
